Why Induction for Inequalities?

Induction is a powerful tool for proving that an inequality holds for all integers greater than or equal to a certain value. The logic is similar to induction for equations, but the algebra often requires careful estimation and manipulation.

The 3-Step Algorithm (for Inequalities)

  1. Base Case: Prove the inequality is true for the starting value (often $n=1$ or $n=4$).
  2. Assumption: Assume the inequality is true for $n=k$.
  3. Inductive Step: Use the assumption to prove the inequality for $n=k+1$.
    *This often requires clever algebra or bounding extra terms!*
